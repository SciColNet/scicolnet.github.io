Government Policies and Scientific Collaboration (GPSC)

A NetSci 2025 Satellite Workshop

This workshop explores the transformative impact of scientific mobility policies on scientific collaboration networks and the flow of knowledge. Using cutting-edge techniques in network science and machine learning, we aim to uncover how these initiatives shape global research dynamics and influence long-term scientific development.

Developing nations often face significant hurdles in enhancing their scientific output, including brain drain and budget limitations. In response, several countries—such as China, India, Indonesia, and Brazil—have introduced scientific mobility policies to provide researchers with international exposure and improve scientific outcomes.

Examples include:

  • China Scholarship Council: Funding for Ph.D. programs and short-term visits to foreign institutes.
  • Indonesian Government Programs: Sponsorships for Ph.D. students to study abroad and return to their home country.
  • India’s SPARC Fellowship: Initiatives fostering foreign collaborations and bilateral exchanges.
  • Brazil’s visiting researcher program
These policies aim to develop expertise, reverse brain drain, and promote global research collaborations.

Our Goals

This workshop focuses on modeling and assessing the long-term impacts of such scientific mobility policies , comparing countries that adopt these policies with those that do not. Key objectives include:

  • Analyzing co-authorship networks and global collaboration dynamics.
  • Understanding shifts in research topics and collaboration patterns.
  • Evaluating the structural and dynamic changes in scientific networks over time.
  • Assessing the long-term effects on a country’s scientific development and individual researchers.
  • Understanding how scientific mobility and return policies affect collaboration patterns.

By combining statistical analyses, network science, and machine learning, we aim to provide actionable insights for policymakers to refine strategies and foster equitable, impactful scientific progress on a global scale.

Join us to delve into these critical questions and contribute to shaping the future of scientific collaboration!
